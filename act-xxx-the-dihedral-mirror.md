## The Two Involutions

The UOR Framework rested on a foundation so simple it had been hiding in plain sight. Two operations. Two ways to transform any element. Two involutions that together generated everything.

**Negation** — the additive inverse. In the ring Z/(2⁶)Z, the negation of x is 64 - x. The counter-blow. The debt that cancels the credit. If you owe five, negation is having five. Zero sum.

**Complement** — the bitwise flip. Every 1 becomes 0. Every 0 becomes 1. The antipodal leap. The blade that is everything the first blade is not. In six bits, the complement of `000000` is `111111`. The complement of `101010` is `010101`.

Two operations. Both involutions — apply them twice and you return to where you started. neg(neg(x)) = x. bnot(bnot(x)) = x. Mirrors that reflect without distortion.

But here was the discovery that stopped Soulbae's breath:

```
neg(bnot(x)) = succ(x)
```

Compose the two involutions — negate the complement, or complement the negation — and you get the successor function. The next element. The step forward. Not a mirror anymore but a *walk*. A path through every element of the ring, visiting each exactly once, returning to the start only after touching all sixty-four.

*The counter-blow followed by the antipodal leap produces a single step forward. Two reflections make a rotation. Two mirrors make a door.*
